Keywords polarity , ezrin , ERM proteins , scaffolding proteins Abstract Microvilli are actin - based structures found on the apical aspect of many ep - ithelial cells . In this review , we discuss different types of microvilli , as well as comparisons with actin - based sensory stereocilia and ﬁlopodia . Much is known about the actin - bundling proteins of these structures ; we summarize recent studies that focus on the components of the microvillar membrane . We pay special attention to mechanisms of membrane microﬁlament at - tachment by the ezrin / radixin / moesin family and regulation of this protein family . We also discuss the NHERF family of scaffolding proteins that are found in microvilli and their role in microvilli regulation . Microvilli on cul - tured cells are not static structures , and their dynamics and those of their components are discussed . mic support for their plasma membrane . With the exception of cilia and ﬂagella , the plasma membrane of animal cells is supported by microﬁlament - based systems . The microﬁlament - based cortex is therefore critical for cell shape determination , with its structure being regulated by signal transduction pathways and its function integrated with exocytic and endocytic membrane trafﬁc . Further , all cells essentially have functionally distinct membrane domains , with each having a characteristic morphology and membrane protein and lipid composition . Thus , a major challenge in cell biology is to understand how cytoskeletal organization , signal transduction , and membrane trafﬁcking pathways collectively generate speciﬁc membrane domains . The classic example is a polarized epithelial cell : Its microvilli - studded apical domain has a morphology and composition distinct from that of the basolateral domain . To understand the way in which cells assemble and regulate membrane domains , model systems have been selected that are easy to study experi - mentally . To date , much information relating to the functional organization of the cell cortex has come from studies initially derived from the red blood cell ( Bennett & Baines 2001 , Bennett & Lorenzo 2013 ) and the brush border of the apical aspect of intestinal epithelial cells . The microvilli on the apical domain of polarized epithelial cells are well - deﬁned , ordered structures , whereas the less - organized microvilli of trophoblasts vary in length . Filopodia , which often exist at the leading edge of migrating cells , are structurally similar to microvilli but are composed of distinct membrane and cytoskeletal proteins . Stereocilia are extremely specialized microvilli that exist on the apical domain of cochlear cells and are organized in a canonical staircase hierarchy . review , we discuss the structure , function , and diversity of microvilli on epithelial cells . For a detailed description of the work upon which this ﬁeld was founded , see previous reviews in this journal ( Bretscher 1991 , Bretscher et al . 2000 , Mooseker 1985 ) . One goal of this review is to dispel the notion that all microvilli are the same . The word microvillus has come to mean a membrane - enclosed , ﬁnger - like cell surface projection that is supported by a core bundle of actin ﬁlaments . This description implies a single type of structure , which is clearly not the case , as microvilli from intestinal epithelial cells and placental syncytiotro - phoblasts have different cytoskeletal , and of course membrane , protein compositions ( Bretscher 1983 , 1989 ) . The giant microvilli of sensory hair cells involved in hearing , unfortunately called stereocilia , also have a different composition and function ( Tilney et al . 1992 ) . Filopodia , associ - ated with the leading edge of migrating cells , have a similar structure but a different composition ( Ahmed et al . 2010 ) ( Figure 1 ) . In this review , we discuss the composition of microvilli and the role and regulation of individual components , relating these to the diversity of function of these structures . STRUCTURAL COMPONENTS OF MICROVILLI Actin The core of the brush border microvillus is a tightly packed bundle of approximately 20 polarized actin ﬁlaments that are oriented with the growing end , deﬁned as the barbed or plus end , facing the plasma membrane at the microvillus tip ; the bundle is composed of both β - and γ - actin isoforms ( Bretscher & Weber 1978 ) . The number of actin ﬁlaments in other microvilli is unknown . In stereocilia , however , the number is > 3 , 000 ( Tilney et al . 1980 ) . The number of ﬁlaments in each structure presumably reﬂects the physical forces it has to endure from the surrounding plasma membrane and the environment . Actin Cross - Linking Proteins Three major and very different actin - binding proteins are responsible for bundling the ﬁlaments in the cores of intestinal microvilli : ﬁmbrin , villin , and espin ( Bartles 1998 ; Bretscher & Weber 1979 , 1980a ) . Fimbrin consists of two calponin homology domains , each of which can bind an actin ﬁlament ( de Arruda et al . 1990 ) , whereas villin consists of six gelsolin - like repeats followed by a C - terminal F - actin – binding headpiece ( Bazari et al . 1988 ; Glenney & Weber 1981 ; Glenney et al . 1980 , 1981 ) . Espin is unique in that it binds actin monomers through a single internal WH2 domain ( Loomisetal . 2003 ) , whichisnotnecessarytobundleF - actinﬁlaments . Espinalonecannot nucleate the assembly of actin ﬁlaments in vitro ( Loomis et al . 2006 ) , so the purpose of potentially increasing the local concentration of actin monomers within the microvilli core through its WH2 domain is not understood . Not all microvilli are compositionally identical , as microvilli from placenta contain the bundling proteins ﬁmbrin and α - actinin but no villin ( Coluccio & Bretscher 1989 ) , whereas stereocilia contain higher levels of espin , as well as ﬁmbrin , but no villin ( Flock et al . 1982 , Sekerkov´a et al . 2006 ) . Villin and ﬁmbrin are the two major actin - bundling proteins of intestinal brush border mi - crovilli ( Bretscher 1983 ) . Although villin is the ﬁrst actin bundler recruited to the apical domain of intestinal epithelia ( Ezzell et al . 1989 ) , atomic modeling and crystal structures of F - actin bundled by ﬁmbrin alone correlate with the 3D structures of the F - actin core seen in electron microscopy images of the intestinal brush border , indicating that the paracrystalline order of the F - actin core is imparted by ﬁmbrin ( Volkmann et al . 2001 ) . Consistent with this view , F - actin bundles that are formed with villin alone are less organized than those bundled with just ﬁmbrin ( Matsudaira et al . 1983 ) . Both actin bundlers are able to bind to F - actin ﬁlaments simultaneously with no compe - tition , suggesting the proteins work together to bundle F - actin in the microvilli ( Hampton et al . 2008 ) . Espin is the least abundant actin - bundling protein and the last to be recruited to the actin core of microvilli ( Bartles 1998 ) . Espin binds to F - actin with a higher afﬁnity than ﬁmbrin and villin do ( Chen et al . 1999 ) , which suggests that it is a more stable and less dynamic actin - bundling protein . Espin , unlike villin , bundles actin ﬁlaments in a calcium - independent manner ( Bartles 1998 ) , which may be important in systems that see large ﬂuctuations in intracellular calcium levels but still need to maintain intact actin bundles . Interestingly , villin not only is an actin - bundling protein but also nucleates the assembly of F - actin ﬁlaments , as well as severs and caps them in a Ca 2 + - dependent manner ( Bretscher & Weber 1980b , Khurana & George 2008 ) . As described below , it is therefore possible that the primary role of villin in microvilli is not to bundle the F - actin core but to regulate the distribution of actin ﬁlaments . Mice in which the villin , ﬁmbrin , or espin gene has been individually knocked out are viable , and in the case of espin ( Zheng et al . 2000 ) and villin ( Ferrary et al . 1999 ) single knockout mice , no defect in microvillar ultrastructure is evident . In the ﬁmbrin knockout mouse , the microvilli are shorter and the actin ﬁlaments are less ordered and lack rootlets , a defect attributed to improper terminal web organization and composition ( Grimm - G¨unter et al . 2009 ) . Compelled by the thought that a fourth bundling protein must exist within the microvilli , Revenu and colleagues ( 2012 ) performed a proteomics analysis on the brush border of these mice . However , they found no obvious candidate protein that could compensate by bundling F - actin ﬁlaments in response to the loss of all three F - actin bundlers . Either a protein exists in such low abundance that it was not detected by this proteomics approach , or other microvillar proteins can compensate , even if actin bundling is not their traditional or primary role . If microvilli can form without espin , ﬁmbrin , and villin , what are other possible roles for these proteins ? As mentioned earlier , espin is a highly abundant protein in stereocilia . In the espin knockout mouse , stereocilia are shorter and quickly degenerate , ultimately resulting in deafness ( Sj¨ostr¨om & Anniko 1992 ) . Overexpression of espin in brush border microvilli results in the lengthening of microvilli in a dose - dependent manner ( Loomis et al . 2003 ) , which suggests that espin plays a role in the length regulation of structures that contain parallel actin bundles . The F - actin – severing activity of villin has recently been demonstrated to be important for directed epithelial cell migration ( George et al . 2013 ) and colonic wound healing in mice ( Ubelmann et al . 2013 ) . Villin , through its F - actin – severing activities , is important for the rapid disassembly of apical microvilli . This leads to an increase in the concentration of available actin , which is then directly incorporated into the leading edge of migrating cells ( Ubelmann et al . 2013 ) . This suggests that villin plays a role in the dynamic turnover of the F - actin core to increase the local concentration of free actin for other functions , such as the epithelial to mesenchymal transition . At the tip of a microvillus is an electron - dense region directly below the plasma membrane ( Tilney 1970 ) . A component of this tip complex is Eps8 , which has the ability to both cap F - actin ﬁlaments and bundle them ( Croce et al . 2004 ) . In the mouse , loss of Eps8 results in shorter brush border microvilli ( Tocchetti et al . 2010 ) , whereas in Caenorhabditis elegans , microvilli elongate in response to loss of Eps8 ( Croce et al . 2004 ) . This discrepancy may be explained by the presence of only a single Eps8 ortholog in C . elegans ; the four members in mammalian systems share functional redundancy ( Offenh¨auseretal . 2004 ) . Inagreementwiththe C . elegans data , overexpressionofEps8 in LLCPK1 cells results in signiﬁcantly shorter microvilli ( Zwaenepoel et al . 2012 ) , suggesting that Eps8 controls the length of microvilli through its ability to cap F - actin ﬁlaments and prevent further monomer addition from occurring . The importance of the bundling activity of Eps8 has yet to be established . Microvilli vesiculate the plasma membrane at the tip of microvilli in a Myo1a - dependent man - ner ( McConnell & Tyska 2007 ) . One possible biological consequence of this function is to further increase the surface area of the intestinal plasma membrane for nutrient absorption . Another pos - sibility is that this vesiculation contributes proteins to the luminal space of the intestine . Vesicles shed from the tips of microvilli are enriched in proteins such as intestinal alkaline phosphatase , an important component of the mucosal barrier of the gastrointestinal tract ( McConnell et al . 2009 ) . Whether this feature of intestinal microvilli occurs in other systems has yet to be determined . Microvilli Length Regulation As mentioned above , the loss of all three microvillar actin - bundling proteins in the knockout mouse results in shorter microvilli , as does overexpression of Eps8 . One can imagine that loss of actin - bundling proteins reduces the stiffness of the core , so it cannot support itself against the force of the plasma membrane beyond a certain length . Interestingly , the microvillar base – localized protein Cordon Bleu has emerged as a potential regulator of microvillar length . Cordon Bleu has an N - terminal COBL domain and three WH2 domains toward the C terminus ( Ahuja et al . 2007 ) . MEMBRANE COMPONENTS OF MICROVILLI Lipids of the Microvillar Membrane It is well established that the inner and outer leaﬂets of the plasma membrane have different lipid compositions and that , for epithelial cells , the outer leaﬂet of the apical membrane has a very different lipid composition from the outer leaﬂet of the basolateral membrane ( Ikonen & Simons 1998 , Mishra et al . 2010 , Schuck & Simons 2004 , Simons & Van Meer 1988 ) . Two lipids in particular , phosphoinositides and sphingolipids , are important for the regulation of the apical membrane . Phosphoinositides have emerged as identity markers for membrane - bound compartments of the secretory and endocytic pathways ( Balla 2013 , Di Paolo & De Camilli 2006 ) . Phosphatidyl - inositol 4 , 5 - bisphosphate ( PIP 2 ) is enriched in the inner leaﬂet of the apical membrane , whereas phosphatidylinositol 3 , 4 , 5 - trisphosphate is found in the inner leaﬂet of the basolateral surface ( Blazer - Yostetal . 2004 , vandenBogaartetal . 2011 ) . PIP 2 isinvolvedinvarioussignalingpathways , inreceptor - mediatedendocytosisthroughcoatedpits ( Antonescuetal . 2011 , McMahon & Boucrot 2011 ) , in non - clathrin - mediated endocytosis ( Yeung et al . 2006 ) , and in the regulation of the actin cytoskeleton . The role of PIP 2 in activation of the microvillar protein ezrin is discussed below . Sphingolipids are structural and metabolic molecules produced in response to a range of ex - tracellular cues , such as cytokines and growth factors ( Hannun & Obeid 2008 ) . Sphingolipids , such as ceramide , are synthesized in the endoplasmic reticulum and constitute a central source for other sphingolipids . Ceramide metabolism generates sphingosine - 1 - phosphate ( S1P ) , which alongside ceramide regulates ezrin , radixin , and moesin ( ERM ) activation and deactivation ( Zeidan et al . 2008 ) . Ceramide inactivates ezrin via a PIP 2 - independent mechanism by the ac - tion of the serine / threonine phosphatase PP1 α ( Canals et al . 2012 ) . Conversely , S1P causes ezrin hyperphosphorylation . This effect may be independent of cell type and receptor - mediated , as ezrin activation via S1P requires S1P receptor 2 ( Gandy et al . 2013 ) . Another sphingolipid , sph - ingomyelin , is enriched in a polar manner in the apical membrane of epithelial cells , where it is organized in clusters and is a prerequisite for microvilli formation ( Ikenouchi et al . 2012 ) . Sphin - gomyelinclustersenableco - clusteringofthesialomucinpodocalyxin - 1 , followedbyrecruitmentof ezrin - radixin - moesin - binding phosphoprotein 50 ( EBP50 ) , which in turn promotes clustering of ezrinandphosphatidylinositol4 - phosphate5 - kinase β ( PIP5K β ) intheapicalmembrane . PIP5K β produces PIP 2 , thus enhancing the activation and accumulation of ezrin and the formation of mi - crovilli ( Ikenouchi et al . 2013 ) . Recent reports indicate that a podocalyxin / EBP50 / ezrin complex is essential for promoting apical identity , by the action of ezrin as a regulator of podocalyxin and EBP50 ( Bryant et al . 2014 ) . CB31CH26 - Bretscher ARI 15 October 2015 15 : 16 Proteomics of the Microvillar Membrane The microvillar membrane adjusts to the physiological needs of a particular cell and often includes proteins involved in the transport of ions and nutrients , hydrolases to enable the processing of nutrients , receptors for ligands that generate intracellular responses , and surface proteins that provide a physical barrier against extracellular challenges . The adaptation to physiological need is nicely demonstrated by the membrane proteome along the human colon ( van der Post & Hansson 2014 ) . The proximal parts are characterized by mem - brane proteins that facilitate metabolism , protein biosynthesis , bacterial sensing , and cation trans - port , whereas membrane proteins involved in lipid metabolism , microbial defense , anion transport and O - glycosylation dominate toward the distal colon . For example , higher levels of N + / H + ex - changer 3 ( NHE3 ) and proteins involved in major histocompatibility complex Class I presentation of antigens are found in proximal rather than distal regions of the colon , corresponding to the absorptive and bacteria - sensing processes that take place there . In another study , the proteome of murine brush borders showed ﬁve major classes of proteins : cytoskeletal proteins , hydrolases , channels / transporters , myosin motor proteins , and adhesion molecules ( McConnell et al . 2011 ) . These classes of proteins are involved in the regulation of actin dynamics , membrane bending , extracellular adhesion , and nutrient and mucus processing . Besides many of the abundant cy - toskeletal proteins , such as actin , villin , ﬁmbrin , and ezrin , many low - abundance proteins of the cytoskeleton were detected . These included radixin , Cordon Bleu , ﬁlamins A and B , tropomyosin , proﬁlin - 1 , and harmonin . Other proteins recently shown to play important roles in the organiza - tion of the brush border are adhesion molecules such as mucin - like protocadherin ( MLPCDH ) and protocadherin - 24 ( PCDH24 ) . The proteome also revealed a total of 14 distinct myosin su - perfamily members , some of which we discuss below . The Microvillar Glycocalyx The glycocalyx was ﬁrst described as a thick ﬁlamentous coat covering the microvillar surface of many epithelial cells , including enterocytes ( Ito 1969 ) . This ﬁlamentous layer consists of sulfated and carboxylated glycoproteins and glycolipids ( Rambourg et al . 1966 ) . The function of the glyco - calyx is still under debate . However , it is clear that glycoproteins extending up to ∼ 1 μ m from the cell surface constitute a dense coat that provides a protective function and diffusion layer as well as the site for a wide range of interactions with molecules in the extracellular space . Several reports indicate that the major components of the glycocalyx are transmembrane mucins ( Kesimer et al . 2013 , Linden et al . 2008 , Pelaseyed et al . 2013a ) . Most transmembrane mucins share a common domain structure : an N - terminal signal sequence , a heavily O - glycosylated mucin domain , an SEA domain , a single - span transmembrane domain , and a C - terminal cytoplasmic tail . The SEA domain is autocatalytically cleaved during mucin biogenesis ; thus , mucins presented on cell surfaces are heterodimers consisting of a cell - attached C - terminal subdomain and an extracellular N - terminal domain harboring the mucin domain . Three intestinal transmembrane mucins , MUC3 , MUC12 , and MUC17 , display conserved PDZ - binding motifs in their cytoplasmic domains . The PDZ - binding motifs allow coregulation of transmembrane mucins with secretory and absorptive events relayed by PDZ proteins , which are suggested to coordinate monitoring of the extracellular environment with secretory events ( Malmberg et al . 2008 , Pelaseyed & Hansson 2011 , Pelaseyed et al . 2013a ) . MICROFILAMENT - MEMBRANE ATTACHMENT A critical feature of microvilli is the lateral attachment of the F - actin core to the plasma membrane ( Figure 2 ) . This attachment has to sustain tension across the epithelium , especially at the base of the microvillus . Much has been discovered about lateral attachment , but the molecules needed to resist tension caused by the strong membrane curvature at the base of the microvillus remain to be discovered . Myosins Ultrastructural links between the actin core and membrane in intestinal microvilli were described in the 1970s and then shown to be made up of a 110 kDa polypeptide with associated calmodulin , later classiﬁed as myosin - 1a ( Brunser & Luft 1970 ; Collins & Borysenko 1984 ; Coluccio 1991 , 1997 ; Coluccio & Bretscher 1987 , 1988 , 1990 ; Conzelman & Mooseker 1987 ; Howe et al . 1982 ; Howe & Mooseker 1983 ; Howe et al . 1980 ; Krizek et al . 1987 ; Matsudaira & Burgess 1979 ; Mooseker & Tilney 1975 ) . Myosin - 1a consists of a motor head domain ; a lever arm with IQ motifs for binding calmodulin and a calmodulin - like protein ; and a C - terminal region , TH1 ( tail homology 1 ) ( McConnell & Tyska 2010 ) . Initially it was shown that myosin - 1a can bind acidic phospholipids , and later it was demonstrated that the TH1 domain binds phosphatidylserine and PIP 2 through different motifs ( Hayden et al . 1990 , Mazerik & Tyska 2012 ) . Recently , the binding of the head domain to actin has been shown to restrict the dynamics of myosin - 1a ( Mazerik et al . 2014 ) , consistent with a microﬁlament - membrane linking function with a high duty ratio ( Jontes et al . 1997 ) . Mice in which the myosin - 1a gene is knocked out are viable and grow normally , although the morphology of the brush border is disturbed , microvilli tend to vesiculate , levels of the enzyme sucrase isomaltase are reduced , and myosin - 1c that is normally targeted to other compartments now localizes to microvilli ( Tyska et al . 2005 ) . Myosin - 1d is found at the tips of intestinal microvilli as well as in the region of the terminal web . The Ezrin , Radixin , and Moesin Protein Family Ezrin , radixin , and moesin are collectively known as the ERM proteins and belong to the band 4 . 1 superfamily , whose members share a common N - terminal FERM domain ( Gould et al . 1989 , Sato et al . 1992 ) ( Figure 3 ) . These interactions , modulated by the conformation state of ezrin and accessibility of the FERM domain , provide the basis for local recruitment of protein networks and link these networks to the cytoskeleton to facilitate remodeling of the cytoskeleton and membranes . REGULATION AND DYNAMICS OF MICROVILLI AND MICROVILLAR COMPONENTS Microvilli on cultured cells are dynamic structures with three phases in their life cycle : ( a ) the generation of microvilli growing at 5 nm / s , ( b ) a steady state of ∼ 5 min , and ( c ) a retraction phase with a rate of 1 . 2 nm / s ( Gorelik et al . 2003 ) . This leads to an average life cycle of 12 . 1 ± 5 . 6 min . The turnover of microvillar proteins has been recognized for a long time ( Stidwill et al . 1984 ) . The F - actin core in the microvilli of Caco - 2 cells at steady state treadmills , with a rate of monomer addition at the barbed end of approximately 1 . 5 – 3 . 0 s − 1 ( Loomis et al . 2003 , Tyska & Mooseker 2002 ) , corresponding to approximately 0 . 2 μ m / min . The protocadherin intermicrovillar link sta - bilizes microvilli of intestinal epithelial cells , resulting in a prolonged steady state ( Crawley et al . 2014 ) . The actin ﬁlaments in the core of stereocilia treadmill more slowly , at 0 . 002 – 0 . 04 subunits s − 1 ( Rzadzinska et al . 2004 ) or hardly at all ( Zhang et al . 2012 ) . What is the mechanism of microvillus assembly , and how does a cell regulate the number of these structures on the apical domain ? An attractive mechanism for microvillus assembly would be the local activation of a formin protein . However , initial attempts to implicate the major formin proteins present in epithelial cells have not yielded any likely candidates ( Wayt & Bretscher 2014 ) . Loss of ERM proteins in sparsely growing cultured cells reduces or eliminates the presence of microvilli ( Takeuchi et al . 1994 , Viswanatha et al . 2012 ) , as does knockdown of the ezrin kinases LOK and SLK ( Viswanatha et al . 2012 ) or EBP50 ( Hanono et al . 2006 ) . However , the loss of ezrin or EBP50 in mice does not eliminate microvilli from intestinal epithelial cells ( Morales et al . 2004 , Saotome et al . 2004 ) . Similarly , knockdown of ERM proteins in a conﬂuent epithelial monolayer has little effect on surface microvilli ( R . Viswanatha , unpublished data ) , suggesting that additional mechanisms stabilize microvilli under these conditions . The molecular basis for these dynamics and their physiological signiﬁcance remain to be uncovered . DISEASES Although misregulation of microvillar proteins such as ezrin and EBP50 has implications for certain cancer states , few diseases have been directly linked to a loss of microvilli phenotype , presumably because having functional microvilli is a prerequisite for life . One example is rotavirus infections , which are well known for causing gastrointestinal disease . Upon infection , the viral VP4 protein binds actin to alter the organization of microvilli on intestinal epithelial cells by remodeling the actin bundles into cytoplasmic actin bodies ( Gardet et al . 2006 ) . This process may also involve the observed increase in intracellular Ca 2 + ( Brunet et al . 2000 ) . In part due to this reorganization , the presence of apical membrane proteins , such as sucrase isomaltase , dipeptidyl peptidase IV , and sodium - glucose cotransporter 1 , is also disrupted by rotavirus infection ( Beau et al . 2007 , Halaihel et al . 2000 , Jourdan et al . 1998 , Ramig 2004 ) . Theonlydocumentedexampleofaninheriteddisorderismicrovillarinclusiondisease ( MVID ) , which is a fatal congenital microvillar disorder that affects infants . Enterocytes from patients with MVID are characterized by shortened or absent apical microvilli , as well as by vacuolar inclusions that contain microvilli ( Ruemmele et al . 2006 ) . PERSPECTIVES Much progress has been made over the past 40 years on understanding microvilli , but many key questions remain . Most attention has been paid to the structural components of microvilli ; prob - ably all of the major proteins have been identiﬁed and characterized . Surprisingly little is known about how cells regulate the location of microvilli , their length , and the protein composition of their membranes . The ezrin phosphocycle provides an interesting mechanism for the restriction of microvilli to the apical domain , but it does not explain how assembly is initiated . The recent report of protocadherin - based linkages between microvillar tips also provides an attractive mech - anism for optimizing microvillar packaging and length regulation in cells with maximally packed microvilli , such as intestinal epithelial cells . But how are length and actin ﬁlament number regu - lated in these systems ? Microvilli in the proximal gut are thinner and longer than in the distal gut . Other important questions include : How does the cell determine which part of the microvillus core is enveloped in membrane ? How is the expected high tension across the base of each mi - crovillus transmitted across the cell ? What proteins are selectively retained in microvilli , and by what mechanisms ? How is the microvillus proteome affected by the cytoskeletal composition of the microvillus ? And ﬁnally , microvilli are usually viewed as structures that enhance surface area for absorption , but do they have additional functions ?
