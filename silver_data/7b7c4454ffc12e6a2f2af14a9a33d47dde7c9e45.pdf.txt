Abstract 23 Background . Human behavior , economic activity , vaccination , and social distancing are 24 inseparably entangled in epidemic management . This study aims to investigate the effects of 25 various parameters such as stay - at - home restrictions , work hours , vaccination and social distance 26 on the containment of pandemics such as COVID - 19 . 27 Methods . To achieve this , we developed an agent - based model based on a time - dynamic graph 28 with stochastic transmission events . The graph is constructed from a real - world social network . 29 The graph ' s edges have been categorized into three categories : home , workplaces , and social 30 environment . The conditions needed to mitigate the spread of wild - type ( WT ) COVID - 19 and the 31 delta variant have been analyzed . Our purposeful agent - based model has carefully executed tens 32 of thousands of individual - based simulations . We propose simple relationships for the trade - offs 33 between effective reproduction number ( R e ) , transmission rate , work hours , vaccination , and stay 34 at home restrictions . 35 Results . For the WT , it has been found that a 13 % increase in vaccination impacts the 36 reproduction number , like the magnitude of decreasing nine hours of work to four and a single 37 day of stay - at - home order . For the delta , 16 % vaccination has the same effect . Also , since we can 38 keep track of household and non - household infections , we observed that the change in household 39 transmission rate does not significantly alter the R e . Household infections are not limited by 40 transmission rate due to the high frequency of connections . For COVID - 19 ' s specifications , the 41 R e depends on the non - household transmissions rate . 42 Conclusions . Vaccination and transmission reduction are almost interchangeable .
