Goals and hypothe - ses lead tosteps of data collection and preparation . Researchers specify an observational process to collect information about the latent phenomena of interest from the environment . An observa - tional probe is used to induce explicit observations thought to be sensitive to the target phenomena . For example , psychologists de - sign human subjects experiments using interventions thought to interact with the target phenomena . ML researchers often make use of datasets generated from human produced media and signals of behavior , in the form of digital traces . An observational process becomes a model by making assump - tions about what the observed data represent , namely realizations ofrandomvariables with regularvariation . The observationalmodel is deÔ¨Åned by a model representation , i . e . , the model class or functional form that speciÔ¨Åes a space of data generating processes ( DGPs , i . e . , Ô¨Åtted functions ) that might have produced the data . High measurement error relative to signal , un - acknowledged Ô¨Çexibility in deÔ¨Åning data inputs , underspeciÔ¨Åed or non - representative subject samples , and underspeciÔ¨Åcation of stim - uli generation , and other ‚Äúdesign freedoms‚Äù can threaten the validity of conclusions drawn in empirical psychology research . The design of many psychology experiments implies that re - searchers do not grasp the implications of using small samples and noisy measurements to draw inferences about eÔ¨Äects that are a pri - ori likely to be small . For example , a thought - to - be pervasive belief is that if an experiment registers a ‚Äústatistically signiÔ¨Åcant‚Äù eÔ¨Äect on a small sample , then that eÔ¨Äect will necessarily remain signiÔ¨Å - cant with a larger sample [ 42 , 192 ] . In reality , with a lower powered study , not only is there a lower probability of Ô¨Ånding a true eÔ¨Äect of a given size , but there is a lower probability that an observed eÔ¨Äect which passes a signiÔ¨Åcance threshold actually reÔ¨Çects a true eÔ¨Äect that will appear under replication [ 42 ] . Under low power , es - timates of observed eÔ¨Äects will tend to reÔ¨Çect sampling error that derives from the limited size of the sample relative to a target pop - ulation , and forms of measurement error [ 141 ] , such as random variation due to noise in taking measurements that produces a dif - ference between observed and true values . Studies are ‚Äúdead on arrival‚Äù when standard error due to measurement and sampling variation is large relative to any plausible eÔ¨Äect size [ 91 ] . Inherent Ô¨Çexibility in how a researcher speciÔ¨Åes an analysis is a diÔ¨Äerent type of threat . Social psychology . Claims made in social psychology research are threatened when researchers treat conventional approaches to model - based inference as a black box for consuming data and out - putting inferences [ 12 , 93 , 136 ] , and by researchers‚Äô implicit use of statistical signiÔ¨Åcance alone as a criterion for deciding what to re - port . It is relatively rare for psychology research contributions to in - clude explicit motivation for the estimators and loss functions used in modeling . Such ‚Äúinference by convention‚Äù can produce mislead - ing claims without outright cheating or motivated reasoning simi - lar to how blindly preferring between - subjects designs can . For ex - ample , conventional use of maximum likelihood estimators based on their consistency [ 217 ] may lead researchers to overlook criti - cal assumptions required for these estimators to be well - calibrated ( i . e . , have sampling distributions which are asymptotically normal ) . Analytical approaches tooptimizationbring convenience , butcommonly - used approaches to model Ô¨Åtting and selection tend to be based on pre - experimental guarantees ( i . e . , before data are collected ) , which cannot guarantee thatthey willbeappropriate ( e . g . , well - calibrated ) on a particular dataset [ 24 , 215 ] . A diÔ¨Äerent source of misleading claims is the use of statisti - cal signiÔ¨Åcance as a coarse objective function . Implicit optimiza - tion for signiÔ¨Åcance , in which researchers are essentially searching through a garden of forking paths for speciÔ¨Åcations that achieve signiÔ¨Åcance as a sort of quasi - optimization approach [ 88 ] , means that conventional interpretations of Ô¨Åtted models and statistical tests on parameter estimates will not hold . At the highest level , bias aÔ¨Äects the published record when researchers decide whether to report results based on the signiÔ¨Åcance levels [ 85 , 185 ] . Using ‚Äústatistical signiÔ¨Åcance‚Äù as an implicit objective does not line up with scientiÔ¨Åc goals ( e . g . , [ 81 , 102 , 120 , 121 , 214 ] ) . Related problems include not acknowledging that as a random variable , ùëù can vary considerably even under idealizedreplication [ 34 , 90 , 97 , 154 , 189 ] , such that the diÔ¨Äerence between signiÔ¨Åcant and not signiÔ¨Åcant is not itself signiÔ¨Åcant . Machine learning . Claims are often made in ML research with - out acknowledging that they depend critically on choices of hyperpa - rameters , initial conditions , and other conÔ¨Åguration details that di - rectlyinÔ¨Çuenceperformanceinnon - convex optimization . Researchers also may exploit Ô¨Çexibility in designing performance comparisons in order to achieve superior performance for their contributed approach relative to alternatives [ 114 , 188 ] . In contrast to loss functions in simple regression models , ML models tend to have high dimensional non - convex loss . While this does not necessarily prevent generalization [ 48 ] it makes solutions like saddle points , which can give the illusion of a satisfactory lo - cal minimum , of greater concern [ 56 , 184 ] . Optimizers‚Äîalgorithms that prescribe how to update parameter values like weights during inference to reduce the value of the objective on the training data‚Äî are critical to the accuracy gains seen in recent years . Some emergent properties have been criticized . Related to shortcut learning , SGD has been shown to exhibit ‚Äúsimplicity bias‚Äù‚Äîa preference for learning simple pre - dictors Ô¨Årst , resulting in neural nets relying exclusively on the sim - plest features , for example , image color and texture , and remain - ing invariant to complex predictive features , for example , object shape [ 130 , 190 ] . Internal and external validity is threatened by leakage‚Äîbroadly , using information from the test data in training‚Äîparalleling the reuse of data for choosing and evaluating a model‚Äôs Ô¨Åt in psychol - ogy . Other issues occur in performance comparisons of models or algorithms . Finally , implicit optimization for good performance results can also occur in ML . Improving performance on benchmark datasets , which have been thought to have caused most major ML research breakthroughs in the last 50 years [ 66 ] , is how researchers show - case improvement in model performance to get published in top conferences and journals [ 172 , 188 ] across ML subareas ( e . g . , [ 61 , 118 , 216 ] ) .
