The 14 emergence of VoCs fueled interest in understanding the potential impact of travel restrictions and 15 surveillance strategies to prevent or delay the early spread of VoCs . We performed phylogenetic 16 analyses and mathematical modeling to study the importation and spread of the VoCs Alpha and Delta 17 in Switzerland in 2020 and 2021 . Using a phylogenetic approach , we estimated 383 - 1 , 038 imports of 18 Alpha and 455 - 1 , 347 imports of Delta into Switzerland . We then used the results from the phylogenetic 19 analysis to parameterize a dynamic transmission that accurately described the subsequent spread of 20 Alpha and Delta . We modeled different counterfactual intervention scenarios to quantify the potential 21 impact of border closures and surveillance of travelers on the spread of Alpha and Delta . We found that 22 implementing border closures after the announcement of VoCs would have been of limited impact to 23 mitigate the spread of VoCs . In contrast , increased surveillance of travelers could prove to be an 24 effective measure for delaying the spread of VoCs in situations where their severity remains unclear . 25 Our study shows how phylogenetic analysis in combination with dynamic transmission models can be 26 used to estimate the number of imported SARS - CoV - 2 variants and the potential impact of different 27 intervention scenarios to inform the public health response during the pandemic . 28 . Variants of concern ( VoCs ) have emerged which 31 are associated with increased transmissibility , immune escape , changes in disease severity , or a 32 combination thereof ( 1 ) . Alpha and Delta emerged in late 2020 and mid 2021 , and their transmission 33 dynamics were influenced by importations , the heterogeneous landscape of naturally acquired and 34 vaccine - elicited immunity and non - pharmaceutical interventions ( NPIs ) such as surveillance of travelers 35 and travel restrictions . It was previously shown that the resumption of travel after lockdowns in summer 36 2020 led to the importation of a new SARS - CoV - 2 variant that fuelled epidemics in countries with low - 37 incidence ( 2 , 3 ) . In winter 2020 / 2021 , Swiss authorities tightened travel restrictions again for travelers 38 from the United Kingdom ( UK ) ( 4 ) , which were followed by overall stricter measures in Switzerland once 39 concerns about the newly detected Alpha variant were communicated . To date , however , the impact of 40 such travel restrictions and surveillance strategies to prevent or delay the introduction of VoCs are not 41 well understood . 42 43 Global genomic surveillance is vital to capture the transmission dynamics of different SARS - CoV - 2 44 variants around the world . Genomic and epidemiological data allow characterization and quantification 45 of the spread of VoCs ( 5 â€“ 9 ) . At the end of 2020 , sequencing efforts helped to characterize the first VoC , 46 Alpha , that was initially detected in the UK , showing a 37 % - 100 % increase in transmissibility over 47 previously circulating variants ( 5 , 7 â€“ 11 ) . In early 2021 , Alpha was continuously displaced by another 48 VoC , Delta , which likely emerged in India ( 12 ) . For Delta , studies have estimated an increased 49 transmissibility compared to previously circulating variants , which were predominantly Alpha in Europe 50 and North America , Beta in South Africa , and Gamma in South America , between 40 % and 167 % ( 8 , 9 ) . 51 VoCs emerge in one place and spread to different countries through travel . Therefore , quantifying the 52 impact of travel and imported SARS - CoV - 2 cases can help guide pandemic responses , particularly prior 53 to wide - spread vaccination . Routinely obtained surveillance data can provide valuable information 54 about the place of exposure , but can be biased ( 13 ) . Leveraging the wealth of genomic data offers the 55 opportunity to integrate an additional level of information to inform the impact of travel and importation 56 events . To try to prevent or slow the import of VoCs , some countries have introduced travel restrictions 57 such as vaccination certificates , testing , or travel bans . The model accounts for superspreading of SARS - CoV - 2 and simulates 201 epidemic trajectories with 1 , 10 , and 100 seeds , which can be interpreted as imports . The branching 202 process was based on a negative binomial distribution to describe the number of secondary cases , with 203 a mean corresponding to R e and overdispersion parameter k ( 13 , 42 , 43 ) . The generation time was 204 sampled from the gamma distribution with a mean of 5 . 2 days and a standard deviation of 1 . 72 days 205 ( 31 ) . For R e of the variant , we randomly drew 10 4 values from a uniform distribution between 1 . 05 to 206 1 . 15 . In the phylogenetic analysis , we found that single importation events happened early and 285 at least several hundred Alpha ( 383 - 1 , 038 ) and Delta ( 455 - 1 , 347 ) cases were introduced into the Swiss 286 SARS - CoV - 2 epidemic during the study period . The integration of these importation events into a 287 transmission model accurately described the subsequent spread of VoCs . We estimated a 41 % and 288 56 % increased transmissibility of Alpha and Delta compared to previously circulating variants , which is 289 in the range of previously reported estimates ( 5 , 7 â€“ 11 ) . Applying our transmission model using 290 counterfactual intervention scenarios showed that only very strict or existing control measures would 291 substantially delay the time to dominance of VoCs . In contrast , implementing border closures after 292 international warnings delayed the time to dominance of VoCs by a few days only . Increased 293 surveillance of travelers - which is less disruptive than border closures - could prove effective for 294 delaying the spread of VoCs in situations where their severity remains unclear . These findings have 295 important implications for informing intervention strategies in the case of newly emerging SARS - CoV - 2 296 VoCs and future pandemic preparedness . 297 298 The major strength of our study is the combination of phylogenetic analyses with a dynamic 299 transmission model . This not only allowed us to estimate the number of imports but also to investigate 300 counterfactual intervention scenarios . During the study period , SARS - CoV - 2 sequencing coverage in 301 Switzerland was relatively high with an average of 14 % of reported cases being sequenced . Our 302 phylogenetic approach to estimate imported variants is standardizable and thus applicable to other 303 countries with similar genomic surveillance and other emerging pathogens . 304 305 Our study also has a number of limitations . First , we used a deterministic transmission model for our 306 main analysis and ignored the potential effects of stochasticity during the importation and early growth 307 phase of VoCs . In the model , we assumed that all imported VoCs enter a deterministic growth trajectory . 308 In contrast , some imported VoCs might not result in continuous transmission chains and go extinct even 309 with R e > 1 . We showed that stochastic effects during importation and the early growth phase can cause 310 a variation in the time to dominance of several weeks . With the phylogenetic analysis we found 311 . There is some evidence that the generation time of Delta is somewhat shorter ( 44 ) , but we 314 do not expect this to affect our results substantially . Third , we did not consider the importation of other 315 variants that could compete with the new VoCs . Since the new VoCs were characterized by an 316 increased transmissibility , we do not expect this assumption to substantially affect the dynamics of the 317 new VoCs replacing previously circulating variants . Fourth , we did not consider co - infection with 318 different variants ( 45 ) . As we focus our analysis on the spread of VoCs and not on their evolution , we 319 do not expect this to affect our results substantially . Fifth , we assumed a constant ascertainment of 320 SARS - CoV - 2 infections of 50 % , which was informed by seroprevalence studies ( 38 , 39 ) . During the 321 study period , test positivity varied and ascertainment might have fluctuated as well . Thus , our 322 transmission model cannot precisely describe the overall number of infections , which was not the 323 objective of our study . In addition , the high variation in genomic sequencing among different countries 324 can influence the estimated number of imports from the phylogenetic analysis . Finally , the phylogenetic 325 analysis has intrinsic limitations due to the limited sequencing coverage , and interpretation during 326 outbreaks requires caution ( 2 , 46 ) . This is specifically true for Alpha , as sequencing in Switzerland was 327 scaled up considerably during 2021 . There is currently no gold standard for estimating imports . In our 328 analysis , we only considered one importation event per cluster , which could lead to an underestimation 329 of imports . To address this issue , we used two approaches to estimate lower and upper bounds on the 330 number of imports . The liberal approach considered any cluster containing Swiss sequences as an 331 importation event . If the sequencing coverage was low , the liberal approach would thus overestimate 332 imports and exports could cause Swiss sequences to be falsely classified as imports . In contrast , the 333 conservative approach considered only the earliest clusters with Swiss sequence as imports , which 334 means that further potential imports were strictly excluded . 335 336 For monitoring the transmission dynamics of SARS - CoV - 2 , it is important to differentiate between 337 locally - acquired and imported infections , particularly in situations when the local incidence of infections 338 is low ( 13 ) . Using genomic 340 data to estimate the number of imports might have the advantage of being less prone to the bias in 341 . Early 351 detection of variants with substantial immune evasion or altered severity can inform policy makers to 352 adjust control measures , vaccination programs , and health systems . For example , several countries 353 imposed controversial travel bans for visitors from South Africa to prevent importation of Omicron ( 48 ) . 354 In our analysis , we found that complete border closures following warnings of VoCs have limited impact 355 and delay their spread by a couple of days only . Hence , travel bans and any time they may buy for 356 certain interventions , such as increasing the uptake of booster vaccines , have to be carefully balanced 357 against the societal and economic costs that accompany them . Similar to our findings , McCrone et al . 358 reported that control measures that had just been introduced in response to warnings about Delta were 359 late , as introductions have already occurred to other countries ( 25 ) . In addition to studying importation 360 and exportation events of VoCs , it is important to better understand the spread of VoCs in the local 361 context . Variations in naturally - acquired and vaccine elicited levels of immunity , local control measures , 362 mobility , and behavior can strongly influence the local spread of VoCs . Taking these factors into account 363 will be critical to inform country - specific strategies to respond to the emergence of new VoCs . 364 365 Integrating phylogenetic analyses with dynamic transmission models can provide critical insights into 366 the importation and early spread of SARS - CoV - 2 VoCs , and how they are impacted by different 367 intervention scenarios . In this study , we showed that border closures would have had a limited impact 368 on the spread of Alpha and Delta in Switzerland . In contrast , increased surveillance of travelers can 369 potentially delay the spread of VoCs by several weeks , which can buy time for health systems to prepare 370 for new epidemic waves . 371 .
