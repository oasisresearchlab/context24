Our observations establish the irrelevance of 21 cytoplasmic MMC and the significance of plasma membrane tension in setting off the 22 cell volume regulation machinery . 23 24 Introduction : 25 The cytoplasm and the lumen of many organelles contain different macromolecules in 26 remarkably high densities . Cytoplasmic protein densities may range from 50 - 400 mg / mL , 27 depending on the cell lineage ( Fulton , 1982 ; Cayley et al . , 1991 ) . Cytoplasmic macromolecules 28 primarily comprise proteins ( 56 % ) , nucleic acids ( 23 % ) , lipids ( 9 % ) , polysaccharides ( 3 % ) 29 ( Kohata and Miyoshi , 2020 ) . Consequently , the fraction of excluded volume of the 30 macromolecules becomes notable compared to the total volume of the cytoplasm . This 31 entropic effect is also described as the thermodynamic state of macromolecular crowding 32 ( MMC ) ( Minton , 1981 ; Ellis , 2001 ) , which affects a host of physicochemical processes in the 33 cytoplasm such as diffusion ( Rashid et al . , 2015 ) , active transport ( Nettesheim et al . , 2020 ) , 34 protein - ligand binding kinetics ( Minton , 2001 ; KÃ¶hn et al . , 2021 ) , enzyme - substrate reaction 35 rates ( Thoke , Bagatolli and Olsen , 2018 ; Wilcox , Chung and Slade , 2021 ) , macromolecular self - 36 assembly ( AndrÃ© and Spruijt , 2020 ; Schreck , Bridstrup and Yuan , 2020 ) , protein folding ( Ã…dÃ©n and 37 Wittung - Stafshede , 2014 ) and post - translational modifications ( Darling and Uversky , 2018 ) , to 38 name a few . Consequently , it led to a hypothesis that cells may regulate their cytoplasm MMC 39 to control biochemical processes . Despite the high relevance of cytoplasmic MMC , the 40 literature lacks experimental study on cellular ability for homeostasis of cytoplasmic MMC ( Lin 41 and Amir , 2018 ) . Thus , more information is needed concerning the temporal dynamics and 42 population heterogeneity of cytoplasmic MMC in eukaryotic cells . Cytoplasmic MMC and cell 43 volume are inversely related ; therefore , MMC is hypothesized to be the thermodynamic 44 condition that cell volume regulation machinery utilizes as a volume sensor ( Minton , Colclasure 45 and Parker , 1992 ; Burg , 2000 ; Al - Habori , 2001 ) . However , other factors , such as the mechanical 46 state of the plasma membrane , can alternatively act as sensors for cell volume regulation 47 ( Strange , 2004 ; Hoffmann , Lambert and Pedersen , 2009 ; Gonzalez et al . , 2018 ; Adar and Safran , 2020 ) . 48 Some of the critical factors that alter MMC in the cytoplasm are translation , degradation of 49 proteins , and cytoskeleton disassembly . It is unclear whether or not cells tightly maintain MMC 50 in its cytoplasm for relatively long durations . As a result , the clustering of 297 cytoplasmic proteins causes the ğ‘Ÿ (cid:3006)(cid:3008)(cid:3007)(cid:3017) to rise above the expected curve ( dotted line ) ( Fig 6A ) . 298 Since Heclin treatment leads to an increase in ğ‘Ÿ (cid:3006)(cid:3008)(cid:3007)(cid:3017) , whereas treatment of Cycloheximide or 299 heat shock causes a decrement ( Fig 2H ) , we investigated if the changes in ğ‘Ÿ (cid:3006)(cid:3008)(cid:3007)(cid:3017) in these 300 conditions is due to a change in crowder concentration or protein clustering . Fig 4E suggests 301 no apparent clustering of cytoplasmic proteins in response to Heclin treatment . Thus , we 302 conclude that Heclin treatment leads to a rise in cytoplasmic MMC . Next , we investigate 303 whether the increase in cytoplasmic MMC in Heclin treated cells triggers RVI . Fig 6B 304 establishes that there is no noticeable change in cell volume in response to increased ( Heclin ) 305 or decreased ( Cycloheximide ) cytoplasmic MMC , demonstrating that changes in MMC in NIH 306 fibroblast cells do not trigger RVI / RVD even until 4 hours of treatment . The cytoskeleton is the 307 crucial component of the cell that decides its shape . We investigate its role in determining cell 308 volume and cytoplasmic MMC . Disassembly of actin causes no noticeable change in cell 309 volume ; however , the cytoplasmic MMC increases ( Fig 6C , Fig 3D ) . The rise in cytoplasmic 310 MMC is not inversely proportional to the associated volume change because disruption of 311 actin filaments leads to a significant increase in macromolecular crowder in the form of 312 monomeric G - actin ( Fig 6C , dotted line ) . This further indicates that change in cytoplasmic 313 MMC caused by disassembly of actin filaments does not trigger RVI to achieve homeostasis 314 of MMC . Interestingly , the disassembly of microtubules causes an increase in cell volume ( Fig 315 6C ) . The associated change in MMC is inversely proportional to the volume change , indicating 316 that Nocodazole treatment causes a change in cytoplasmic volume , and cells ignore the 317 associated change in MMC . Cell volume changes simultaneously perturb plasma membrane 318 organization ( Roffay et al . , 2021 ) . To investigate the role of the plasma membrane behind cell 319 volume regulation , we postulate the involvement of TNFR1 ( Tumour Necrosis Factor Receptor 320 1 ) , as it is reported to exhibit enhanced clustering and internalization during hypertonic stress 321 ( Rosette and Karin , 1996 ) . TNFR1 is also known to localize in membrane caveolae ( Dâ€™Alessio 322 et al . , 2010 ; Bae et al . , 2019 ) , and caveolae are the excess membrane folds responsible for 323 mitigating membrane tension imbalances ( Sinha et al . , 2011 ; Roffay et al . , 2021 ) . Moreover , 324 TNFR1 is upstream of NFÎºÎ² , which shows enhanced activity during hypertonic stress after 3 - 325 5h of stress induction ( Roth et al . , 2010 ; Farabaugh et al . , 2020 ) . Zafirlukast , a cysteinyl 326 leukotriene antagonist ( Zhou et al . , 2019 ) , is reported to inhibit TNFR1 clustering ( Weinelt et al . , 327 2021 ) . Zafirlukast treatment abrogates RVI in NIH / 3T3 in response to a hypertonic shock of 328 500mOsm ( Fig 6D ) . In addition , Zafirlukast treatment leads to arrest of cell volume regulatory 329 activity during spreading ( Fig 6E - F ) and microtubule depolymerized state ( Fig 6G ) . To 330 understand the effect of Zafirlukast treatment on the plasma membrane , we compare the 331 membrane - fluctuation tension of control cells with that of Zafirlukast treated using interference 332 reflection microscopy ( IRM ) ( Fig 6H ) ( Biswas , Alex , and Sinha , 2017 ) . Membrane fluctuation - 333 tension closely follows membrane frame tension for an extensive range of tension values 334 ( Shiba , Noguchi , and Fournier , 2016 ) . Fig 6H - J establishes that Zafirlukast treatment causes 335 a rise in membrane tension . The amplitude of temporal fluctuations is decreased in cells 336 treated with Zafirlukast ( Fig 6I ) . Thus , increased membrane tension ( Fig 6J ) deprives the cells 337 of their ability to trigger regulatory volume change in hypertonic , spreading , and microtubule 338 depolymerized states . 339 340 Conclusion 341 Measurement of ğ‘Ÿ (cid:3006)(cid:3008)(cid:3007)(cid:3017) in the cytoplasm and solutions of different BSA concentrations establish 342 that ğ‘Ÿ (cid:3006)(cid:3008)(cid:3007)(cid:3017) is a reliable indicator of macromolecular crowding in cells . MMC lowers ğœ and 343 increases ğ‘Ÿ (cid:2868) of EGFP giving a linear relation between ğ‘Ÿ (cid:3006)(cid:3008)(cid:3007)(cid:3017) and the concentration of the 344 macromolecular crowder . We establish that cells of different lineages maintain spatially 345 inhomogeneous and distinctly unique levels of average cytoplasmic MMC . The average 346 cytoplasmic MMC in NIH - 3T3 cells is maintained tightly for a relatively long time ( at least 8h ) 347 if the spread area does not change significantly . Since cell - volume and cytoplasmic MMC are 348 inversely related , measurement of ğ‘Ÿ (cid:3006)(cid:3008)(cid:3007)(cid:3017) and cell volume allows us to explore the relation 349 between cytoplasmic MMC and cell volume regulation . We find that heat - shock , trypsin - 350 induced contraction , treatment of Cytochalasin D , Heclin , or Cycloheximide shifts the MMC in 351 the cytoplasm without any change in cell volume . This observation establishes that the cells 352 lack the ability for homeostasis of cytoplasmic MMC . Though the shift in MMC can alter a host 353 of biochemical processes in the cytoplasm , NIH / 3T3 cells lack the capabilities to regulate it . 354 Imbalance in extracellular osmolarity is known to trigger RVI / RVD . Our manuscript identifies 355 additional conditions not linked to osmolarity shift , such as cell - spreading and microtubule - 356 depolymerized state of the cells in which cells invoke volume change autonomously . In these 357 conditions , cells enforce volume changes , disregarding cytoplasmic MMC changes . Moreover , 358 the volume and MMC are inversely related . Overall , our observations indicate that MMC does 359 not act as a sensor for autonomous volume change by the cells . Next , we identified TNFR1 360 as a vital player required for invoking cell volume regulation . Inactivation of TNFR1 with 361 Zafirlukast abrogates cellular ability for volume regulation . Having ruled out the role of 362 cytoplasmic MMC in cell volume regulation , we studied the plasma membrane ' s mechanical 363 state ( tension ) in control and Zafirlukast treated cells . Zafirlukast treatment causes an increase 364 in membrane tension and abolishes the ability of the cells to regulate their volume . Thus , this 365 manuscript conclusively rules out the role of cytoplasmic MMC in sensing the cell volume . 366 Instead , membrane tension plays a crucial role in activating cell volume regulatory apparatus 367 in NIH / 3T3 cells . 368 Materials and methods 369 370 Cell culture and pharmacological studies 371 NIH / 3T3 cell line was procured from NCCS ( National Center for Cell Science , Pune , India ) . 372 RAW 264 . 7 cell line was a generous gift from Dr .
