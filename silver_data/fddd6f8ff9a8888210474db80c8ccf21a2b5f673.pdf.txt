The Drosophila neuromuscular junction ( NMJ ) has proven 24 to be a useful system for deciphering the role of Wg in directing activity - dependent synaptic 25 plasticity , which , in the motoneuron , has been shown to be dependent on both the canonical and 26 the noncanonical calcium Wg pathways . Here we show that the noncanonical planar cell polarity 27 ( PCP ) pathway is an essential component of the Wg signaling system controlling plasticity at the 28 motoneuron synapse . We present evidence that disturbing the PCP pathway leads to a 29 perturbation in activity - dependent synaptic plasticity ( ADSP ) . We first show that a PCP - specific 30 allele of dishevelled ( dsh ) affects the de novo synaptic structures produced during ADSP . We 31 then show that the Rho GTPases downstream of Dsh in the PCP pathway are also involved in 32 regulating the morphological changes that take place after repeated stimulation . Finally , we show 33 that Jun kinase is essential for this phenomenon , whereas we found no indication of the 34 involvement of the transcription factor complex AP1 ( Jun / Fos ) . This work shows the 35 involvement of the neuronal PCP signaling pathway in supporting ADSP . Because we find that 36 AP1 mutants can perform ADSP adequately , we hypothesize that , upon Wg activation , the Rho 37 GTPases and Jun kinase are involved locally at the synapse , in instructing cytoskeletal dynamics 38 responsible for the appearance of the morphological changes occurring during ADSP . 39 40 41 Introduction 42 Synapses are the site of plastic events that shape their morphological and 43 electrophysiological properties depending on their experiences ( Harris & Littleton , 2015 ; Ho et 44 al . , 2011 ; Holtmaat & Svoboda , 2009 ; Pereda , 2014 ; Shepherd , 2004 ) . One such plastic event is 45 activity - dependent synaptic plasticity ( ADSP ) , by which the synaptic efficacy can be increased 46 or decreased , reflecting previous excitatory or inhibitory stimuli ( Castillo , 2012 ; Lüscher & 47 Malenka , 2012 ) . This plasticity is thought to be the cellular correlate of learning and memory , 48 and it is essential to improve our understanding of the molecules and molecular signals 49 underlying this phenomenon . The secreted molecule Wg / Wnt regulates ADSP ( Budnik & 50 Salinas , 2011 ; Rosso et al . , 2013 ) . Indeed , activity - dependent mechanisms induce Wnt release 51 that , in turn , induces plasticity signaling in hippocampal neurons ( Chen et al . , 2006 ; Tabatadze et 52 al . , 2014 ) . This increase in Wnt secretion provokes structural alterations in dendritic 53 arborizations ( Ferrari et al . , 2018 ; Wayman et al . , 2006 ; Yu & Malenka , 2003 ) and spines ( Ciani 54 et al . , 2011 ; McLeod et al . , 2018 ; Tabatadze et al . , 2014 ) , promoting changes in synaptic strength 55 and plasticity . Drosophila served as a model of choice to explore the instrumental role of Wg in 56 directing ADSP ( Bai & Suzuki , 2020 ; Budnik & Salinas , 2011 ) . ADSP can be elicited at the 57 NMJ when the preparation is submitted to a repeated stimulation protocol ( Ataman et al . , 2008 ; 58 Maldonado - Díaz et al . , 2021 ; Piccioli & Littleton , 2014 ; Vasin et al . , 2014 , 2019 ) , similar to the 59 one used on hippocampal neurons to elicit LTP ( Wu et al . , 2001 ) . The changes associated with 60 the NMJ experiencing ADSP are both electrophysiological and structural ( Alicea et al . , 2017 ; 61 Ataman et al . , 2008 ; Maldonado - Díaz et al . , 2021 ) . The structural changes consist of the 62 formation of de - novo synaptic structures named “ghost boutons” ( Fig . 1A ) , and the Wg 63 canonical pathway is essential for this plasticity ( Ataman et al . , 2008 ) . Repeated stimulation of 64 the NMJ promotes an increase in synaptic Wg that leads to rapid structural and 65 electrophysiological changes ( Ataman et al . , 2008 ) . In fact , Wg signaling regulates these changes 66 bidirectionally ; post - synaptically through nuclear import of its receptor , and pre - synaptically by 67 the inhibition of GSK3ß / Sgg ( Ataman et al . , 2008 ) . In addition , there is evidence that the 68 calcium pathway is involved in this plasticity . In this pathway , an increase in intracellular 69 calcium activates CaMKII and the transcription factor NFAT ( Koles & Budnik , 2012 ) . The 70 neuronal expression of NFAT subunit A at the NMJ prevents the formation of ghost boutons 71 upon stimulation ( Freeman et al . , 2011 ) . In addition , synaptic CaMKII protein expression 72 increases upon stimulation which correlates with de novo bouton formation ( Nesler et al . , 2016 ) . 73 However , to date the third pathway that responds to the Wg / Wnt signal , the planar cell polarity 74 ( PCP ) pathway , has not yet been assessed for its role in regulating ADSP . 75 Here we use the Drosophila NMJ model to ask whether the Wg PCP pathway ( Fig . 1E ) is 76 involved in ADSP . We use the appearance of de novo synaptic structures , ghost boutons , to 77 quantify the extent of these changes . Because of their rapid development ( 90 min following 78 stimulation ) , ghost boutons represent early pre - synaptic structures that are devoid of post - 79 synaptic differentiation ( Fig . 1A ) . This characteristic has been widely used to easily identify 80 ghost boutons at the NMJ , in order to quantify ADSP ( Alicea et al . , 2017 ; Ataman et al . , 2008 ; 81 Freeman et al . , 2011 ; Fuentes - Medel et al . , 2009 ; Maldonado - Díaz et al . , 2021 ; Nesler et al . , 82 2013 , 2016 ; Piccioli & Littleton , 2014 ; Vasin et al . , 2014 , 2019 ) . Our present work investigates 83 the role of the PCP pathway in the production of these morphological synaptic changes . We find 84 that several pathway members are involved in this process . Indeed , we first show that a PCP - 85 specific dsh allele ( Perrimon & Mahowald , 1987 ) is necessary for optimal ADSP . We then show 86 that activity of the Rho , Rac1 , and Cdc42 GTPases can also influence ADSP . We then 87 investigate the Jun kinase ( JNK ) , an effector of these GTPases ( Coso et al . , 1995 ; Rosso et al . , 88 2005 ) and show that its loss of function is detrimental to ADSP ; however , the transcription factor 89 AP1 ( Fos / Jun ) , a target of JNK ( Etter et al . , 2005 ; Sanyal et al . , 2002 ) , is not necessary for this 90 process . In most of our experiments , we use an inducible RNA expression system to selectively 91 down - or up - regulate the activity of the members of the PCP pathway pre - synaptically only 92 ( post - synaptic expression is unchanged ) and late in development ( after the establishment of the 93 NMJ ) . This allows us to conclude that the effects we observed are due to a change in the PCP 94 pathway activity within the motoneuron and not a consequence of developmental perturbation . 95 On the contrary , we argue that it reflects a need for the PCP pathway within the motoneuron to 96 achieve optimal ADSP . 97 98 Material and Methods 99 Genetics : 100 For experiments using the elav c155 - gal4 insertion on the X chromosome , only females 101 were used ( in order to consider heterozygote animals ) . Because 168 Disheveled ( Dsh ) is the cytoplasmic molecule making the link between the Wg receptor and its 169 different effectors , it is often called the Wg hub ( Gao & Chen , 2010 ) . We asked whether Dsh 170 loss of function could reduce ADSP and phenocopy the defects in ADSP observed in the wg and 171 fz2 loss of function backgrounds ( Alicea et al . , 2017 ; Ataman et al . , 2008 ) . We used a well - 172 described method to repeatedly stimulate the NMJ preparation in a way reminiscent of the 173 stimulation protocol used on hippocampal neurons in culture ( Wu et al . , 2001 ) . In these 174 conditions , after a 90 - min patterned repeated stimulation protocol , we observe the appearance of 175 ghost boutons ( see schematic diagram Fig . 1A ; Ataman et al . , 2008 ) that show the neuronal 176 membrane - specific marker revealed by the anti - HRP immunoreactivity ( Jan & Jan , 1982 ) , 177 without the apposition of the post - synaptic marker Dlg ( Disc large , the membrane - associated 178 guanylate kinase homolog ; Budnik et al . , 1996 ; Lahey et al . , 1994 ) . These de - novo synaptic 179 structures are noticeable when comparing unstimulated control synapses ( Fig . 1B ) to stimulated 180 control synapses ( Fig 1C ; arrowheads point to ghost boutons ) and are easily quantifiable ( Fig 1F - 181 M ) . 182 Throughout this manuscript , we paid attention to the status of the synapse at rest ( without 183 stimulation ) to ensure that there was no significant difference in the mean synaptic ghost bouton 184 number between the studied genetic backgrounds . This way , we can infer that any observed 185 differences after stimulation reflect a change in the synaptic answer to the activity - dependent 186 process . In fact , unstimulated preparations across all the genotypes studied ( Fig 1 F , H , J , L ) 187 show no differences . We later ensured that our stimulation protocol was efficient in eliciting 188 ghost boutons . Here , our unstimulated control preparations showed a mean of 0 . 36 ± 0 . 15 ghost 189 boutons per synapse ( Fig . 1F , black circle , n = 11 ) , and our stimulated controls showed 7 . 28 ± 190 0 . 69 ghost boutons ( Fig 1G , black circle , n = 25 ) , indicating an increase in ghost boutons 191 comparable to previously published work ( Alicea et al . , 2017 ; Ataman et al . , 2008 ; Freeman et 192 al . , 2011 ; Lee et al . , 2017 ; Maldonado - Díaz et al . , 2021 ; Nesler et al . , 2013 , 2016 ; Piccioli & 193 Littleton , 2014 ; Vasin et al . , 2014 , 2019 ) . 194 We then asked whether Dsh was necessary to perform ADSP . Because of the importance 195 of Dsh in transducing the Wg signal , its null alleles ( for example dsh X788 and dsh nYn234Y ) are 196 embryonic lethal and therefore do not allow the study of the larval NMJ ( Klingensmith et al . , 197 1994 ; Perrimon & Mahowald , 1987 ) . To create a viable loss of function condition for dsh , we 198 used RNA interference ( RNAi ; Piccin et al . , 2001 ) and expressed it using the elav - Gal4 post - 199 mitotic neuronal driver ( Robinow & White , 1991 ) . When these preparations were submitted to 200 the patterned repeated stimulation protocol , they showed fewer ghost boutons ( Fig 1 . G , red 201 circles ) . The mean ghost bouton number in these preparations was 4 . 46 ± 2 . 48 , a value less than 202 the control value ( p = 0 . 0026 ; n = 22 ) , suggesting that Dsh is indeed involved in ADSP . 203 Although we used the postmitotic driver elav - Gal4 to initiate dsh loss of function , its 204 expression starts early in development ( end of embryogenesis ) . Therefore , the phenotype we 205 observe could be caused by a detrimental event during synapse formation due to a lack of Dsh 206 activity . To rule out this possibility , we first examined the NMJ and did not notice any phenotype 207 affecting axon routing or synaptic growth . Secondly , we designed an inducible expression 208 system to affect Dsh activity . This system involves the expression of 3 different transgenes 209 within the studied animal : the elav - Gal4 driver , the effector UAS - dsh - RNAi , and the inhibitor 210 Tub - Gal80 TS . In this system , the temperature - sensitive ( TS ) Gal80 TS protein is expressed 211 ubiquitously ( under the control of the tubulin promoter ) and able to repress Gal4 at permissive 212 temperature ( 18°C ; McGuire et al . , 2004 ) . Indeed , at this temperature , the expression of dsh 213 RNAi is shut off and we observe no difference between the controls and the animals containing 214 dsh RNAi in both non - stimulated ( Fig . 1H ) and stimulated ( Fig . 1I ) preparations . At the 215 restrictive temperature ( 29°C ) , the temperature - sensitive mutation carried by Gal80 TS is revealed , 216 producing a non - functional Gal80 , which leads to the de - repression of the system , and the 217 expression of dsh RNAi . This allele , often referred to as a PCP - specific allele , encodes a 234 version of Dsh that selectively affects the transduction of the Wg signaling to the PCP pathway 235 without altering the efficacy of the Wg signal toward the canonical or the calcium pathway 236 ( Axelrod et al . , 1998 ; Boutros et al . , 1998 ; Yanfeng et al . , 2011 ) . To consolidate the idea that the Wg PCP 243 pathway is an integral part of ADSP machinery , we decided to ask whether other members of 244 this pathway ( Fig . 1 E ) could affect this plasticity . 245 246 Rho1 activity can repress the morphological changes associated with ADSP . 247 Since Dsh transduces the Wg signal through several GTPases ( Boutros et al . , 1998 ; Fanto 248 et al . , 2000 ; Schlessinger et al . , 2007 ) , we decided to investigate the role of Rho1 in ADSP . Rho1 249 is a small GTPase that functions as a molecular switch cycling between an inactive GDP - bound 250 form and an active GTP - bound form to mainly regulate the assembly and reorganization of the 251 actin cytoskeleton controlling different cellular activities ( Jaffe & Hall , 2005 ) . In this way , Rho1 252 influences several biological processes such as embryogenesis , cell polarity , and cell division 253 ( Barrett et al . , 1997 ; Hariharan et al . , 1995 ; Olson et al . , 1995 ; Strutt et al . , 1997 ) . Its role in 254 neuronal development has also been studied ; Rho activation can promote or inhibit neuronal 255 growth cones as well as inhibit dendritic growth ( Auer et al . , 2011 ; Dickson , 2001 ; Luo , 2000 ) . 256 Reducing the activity of p190 RhoGAP , a negative regulator of Rho , provokes axonal guidance 257 defects and decreases axon number in hippocampal cells ( Brouns et al . , 2001 ) . In Drosophila 258 mushroom bodies , the reduction of p190 RhoGAP promotes retraction of axonal branching , 259 while its overexpression promotes axonal extension ( Billuart et al . , 2001 ) . In addition , inhibition 260 of neural activity in cortical neurons decreases active Rho levels while expression of 261 constitutively active Rho increases axon branching by elimination and addition of branches 262 ( Ohnami et al . , 2008 ) . 263 Here we asked whether Rho1 was involved in ADSP at the NMJ . Because of Rho1’s 264 multiple cellular functions its null mutations are lethal , so we therefore decided to express a 265 dominant negative version ( Rho1 DN , see material and methods ) and a constitutively active 266 version ( Rho1 CA , see material and methods ) . We used the Gal4 / Gal80 TS inducible system 267 described previously to bypass the potential effects of these constructs’ expression on axon 268 pathfinding and neuronal growth . Although it seems counterintuitive that the expression of both the dominant negative and the 332 constitutively active forms of Rac1 provokes the same phenotype it is reminiscent of phenotypes 333 previously observed during axon pathfinding within neurons expressing Rac1 DN or Rac1 CA . 334 Indeed , ISNb axons of motoneurons expressing either Rac1 DN or Rac1 CA during embryonic 335 development showed arrested growth cones ( Kaufmann et al . , 1998 ) . Interestingly , that study showed that Rac1 CA expression induced a stronger 338 phenotype and an accumulation of F - actin that was not present in Rac1 DN - expressing animals 339 ( Luo et al . , 1994 ) , suggesting that Rac1 CA and Rac1 DN expression led to the same phenotype by 340 affecting different cytoskeletal processes . Here we 342 show that changing Rac1 activity increases the morphological changes associated with activity - 343 dependent synaptic plasticity . Reduction of Wg and its receptor Fz2 impaired the 379 formation of activity - dependent modifications of synaptic structure ( Alicea et al . , 2017 ; Ataman 380 et al . , 2008 ) . Overexpression of GSK3 β / Sgg , an important canonical pathway member , prevents 381 ADSP at the Drosophila NMJ ( Ataman et al . , 2008 ) . Upon stimulation , Wg protein release 382 increases at the synapse ( Ataman et al . , 2008 ) , which suggests an increased activation of Wg 383 signaling pathways . Repeated simulation of the synapse also promotes an increase of CaMKII , 384 and reducing synaptic CaMKII impairs the activity - dependent formation of de novo boutons 385 ( Nesler et al . , 2016 ) . This suggests some role of the Wnt / Ca 2 + non - canonical pathway on 386 activity - dependent synaptic plasticity . 387 The Wg signal for polarity has been described extensively in several cellular 388 environments ( Maung & Jenny , 2011 ) . In the developing eye , alteration of the PCP pathway 389 provokes defects in the anterior - posterior and dorsal - ventral axes in the arrangement of 390 ommatidia ( Adler , 2002 ; Boutros et al . , 1998 ; Fanto et al . , 2000 ; Strutt et al . , 1997 ; Strutt et al . , 391 2013 ) . In the Drosophila wing , the orientation of cellular hairs in the proximal - distal axis is 392 impaired by defects of the PCP pathway ( Adler , 2002 ; Fagan et al . , 2014 ; Strutt et al . , 1997 ; Wu 393 et al . , 2013 ) . Neurons are one of the most morphologically polarized cell types , and mutations of 394 Wnt / PCP pathway members like Dsh , Prickle , Strabismus , and Fz provoke axonal branching 395 defects in mushroom bodies ( MB ) of Drosophila adult brain ( Ng , 2012 ) . Studies showed that 396 Rac GTPase inactivation in mushroom bodies results in axonal branching defects ( Ng et al . , 397 2002 ) . Wnt5 , Fz , and Dsh act in concert with the small GTPase Rac1 to activate the actin 398 assembly functions of dDAAM ( dsh - associated activator of morphogenesis ) necessary for the 399 correct targeting of MB axons ( Gombos et al . , 2015 ) . In addition , dDAAM regulates microtubule 400 stability and synaptic growth at the Drosophila NMJ ( Migh et al . , 2018 ) while expression of 401 Rac DN ( Park et al . , 2022 ) and Rac CA ( Ball et al . , 2010 ) promote synaptic overgrowth at the NMJ . 402 Despite this interest in the Wnt / Wg PCP pathway , its role regarding processes of 403 synaptic plasticity has not been extensively investigated . Here , we have provided evidence that 404 key molecules involved in the PCP pathway can affect the morphological modifications 405 consequent to ADSP and are therefore necessary to achieve optimal activity - dependent synaptic 406 plasticity . It is important to note that we never observed a complete ghost bouton elimination 407 after affecting the PCP pathway . This makes sense since the other Wg pathways — the canonical 408 pathway ( Alicea et al . , 2017 ; Ataman et al . , 2008 ) and the calcium pathway ( Freeman et al . , 409 2011 ; Nesler et al . , 2016 ) — are also necessary to achieve full activity - dependent synaptic 410 plasticity . The contribution of the Wg PCP pathway to the formation of de novo synaptic 411 structures after stimulation illustrates the redundancy and robustness of the Wg / Wnt signal in 412 directing activity - dependent synaptic plasticity . 413 Our work revealed PCP pathway molecules exercising both activation and repression of 414 activity - dependent synaptic plasticity . Indeed , the loss of function of dsh , either using RNAi or 415 the PCP - specific allele dsh 1 , showed compromised plasticity . The same was true for the loss of 416 function of jnk . This suggests that the role of these molecules is to promote activity . Activation 417 and repression of ghost bouton formation was also achieved by modifying Rho GTPase activity . 418 Expression of the constitutively active forms of the three GTPases we tested showed phenotypes , 419 suggesting that these molecules can regulate ADSP . While Cdc42 CA and Rho1 CA expressing 420 synapses show reduced plasticity , Rac1 CA expression elicited an over plastic response . This 421 antagonistic relationship between Rho1 and Rac1 has been documented previously . For example , 422 Rac1 activation promotes neurite formation and outgrowth , but Rho1 activation leads to neurite 423 retraction and suppresses neurite outgrowth ( Lorenzetto et al . , 2013 ; Luo , 2000 ) . Similarly , 424 proper dendritic spine morphogenesis requires balanced Rho GTPase activity regulation ( Penzes 425 & Cahill , 2012 ) . Interestingly , Rac1 induces the formation and maintenance of dendritic spines , 426 while Rho1 decreases spine formation by promoting spine pruning or retraction ( Bolognin et al . , 427 2014 ; Nakayama et al . , 2000 ; Newey et al . , 2005 ; Tashiro et al . , 2000 ) . 428 The observed Cdc42 phenotypes indicate a role in repressing activity - dependent synaptic 429 plasticity . Indeed , the dominant negative form is over plastic , while the constitutively active form 430 is under plastic . This shows that in our system Cdc42 represses ghost bouton formation . This is 431 surprising since most studies show that Cdc42 and the Rho GTPases in general promote 432 membrane protrusion ( Sadok & Marshall , 2014 ) . For example , Rac1 and Cdc42 both promote 433 dendritic spine formation ( Bolognin et al . , 2014 ; Nakayama & Luo , 2000 ) , cell extension 434 ( Nakayama et al . , 2000 ; Swetman et al . , 2002 ) , and actin polymerization ( Lamarche et al . , 1996 ; 435 Nobes & Hall , 1995 ) . At the NMJ , cdc42 mutations provoke an overgrown synapse , suggesting 436 that Cdc42 represses synaptic growth ( Rodal et al . , 2008 ) while expression of Rac DN ( Park et al . , 437 2022 ) , Rac CA and Rac OE ( Ball et al . , 2010 ) promotes synaptic growth . These studies might 438 indicate a more complex interaction between the Rho GTPases . 439 While Rho1 CA animals showed repressed activity - dependent synaptic plasticity , Rho1 DN 440 did not show any phenotype . It is important to note that we know that Rho1 DN expression can 441 decrease Rho function since its constitutive expression creates lethality . We can therefore 442 hypothesize that the absence of phenotype in the Rho1 DN animals might be due to 443 compensation / redundancy from other GTPase activity . Alternatively , its function at the time at 444 which we express its dominant negative form is perhaps not to repress activity - dependent 445 synaptic plasticity . Indeed , Rho GTPases serve different functions at different times . For 446 example , in hippocampal neurogenesis , Cdc42 promotes initial dendritic development and spine 447 maturation , while Rac1 is essential for the late stages of dendritic growth and spine maturation 448 ( Vadodaria et al . , 2013 ) . 449 Finally , both the constitutive and dominant negative forms of Rac1 provoke over 450 plasticity at the NMJ . Although surprising , this is not the first time that the same phenotype has 451 been observed when expressing these two constructs . Expression of both constitutively active 452 and dominant - negative forms of Rac1 causes axonal loss in Drosophila ( Luo et al . , 1994 ) , and 453 Rac1 can have a dual function regulating attractive and repulsive signals in axonal guidance ( Fan 454 et al . , 2003 ) . Taken together , these results give an image of an intricate and complex local role of 455 the Rho GTPases in regulating ADSP induced at the synapse upon high neuronal activity . 456 We have shown that the transcriptional regulator AP1 is unlikely to be involved in 457 activity - dependent synaptic plasticity . We favor the hypothesis that regulation of the 458 cytoskeleton by the PCP pathway is involved in regulating this plasticity . The reorganization of 459 the synaptic cytoskeleton has been extensively studied in spines and is linked to processes of 460 activity - dependent plasticity and learning and memory ( Cornelius et al . , 2021 ; Macgillavry et al . , 461 2016 ; Repetto et al . , 2014 ) . This process involves the reorganization of filamentous actin , actin 462 regulators , and microtubules ( Cornelius et al . , 2021 ; Ka & Kim , 2016 ; Schätzle et al . , 2018 ) . In 463 fact , we have previously isolated one such regulator , the actin regulator Cortactin ( Weaver et al . , 464 2001 ; Weed et al . , 2000 ) and showed that it is a regulator of activity - dependent synaptic 465 plasticity , and that its protein is upregulated under the control of Wg signaling ( Alicea et al . , 466 2017 ) .
