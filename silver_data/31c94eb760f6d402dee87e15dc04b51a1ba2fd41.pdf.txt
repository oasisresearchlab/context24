Nuclear compression - mediated DNA damage drives ATR - dependent Lamin expression and mouse ESC differentiation Tanusri Roy 1 , Niyati Piplani 1 , Lakshmi Kavitha Sthanam 1 , Swetlana Ghosh 1 , Niharika Tiwary 2 , Sayak Dhar 1 , W . Chandra Shekar 2 , Shamik Sen 1 * 1 Department of Biosciences & Bioengineering , IIT Bombay , Mumbai , India 2 Centre for Cellular and Molecular Biology , Hyderabad , India * Correspondence : shamiks @ iitb . ac . in Abstract : Embryonic stem cells ( ESCs ) which are susceptible to DNA damage depend on a robust and highly efficient DNA damage response ( DDR ) mechanism for their survival . However , the implications of physical force - mediated DNA damage on ESC fate remains unclear . We show that stiffness - dependent spreading of mouse ESCs ( mESCs ) induces DNA damage through nuclear compression , with DNA damage causing differentiation through early induction of Lamin A / C expression . Interestingly , differentiation is associated with rescue of DNA damage and activation of the DDR factor ATR . While ATR is typically known to play roles in DDR pathway , its role during stiffness - mediated nuclear compression and mESC differentiation is unknown . Nuclear enrichment of activated ATR on stiff substrates and reduction of Lamin A / C expression upon ATR inhibition suggests that mESC differentiation is driven by nuclear compression - mediated DNA damage and involves ATR - dependent modulation of Lamin A / C . Keywords : ESCs , stiffness , Lamin A / C , ATR , nuclear compression , DNA damage , differentiation . Introduction Embryonic stem cells ( ESCs ) isolated from the inner cell mass of a late blastocyst of a developing embryo 1 , serve as an important tool for studying pluripotency and development apart from stem cell - based therapies and genetic engineering 2 . Due to their high rate of proliferation and a shorter G1 phase 3 , 4 , ESCs are particularly susceptible to DNA damage , and depend on a robust and highly efficient DNA damage response ( DDR ) mechanism to maintain their self - renewability and pluripotency 5 . ATM ( Ataxia telangiectasia mutated ) and ATR ( ATM and Rad3 - related ) are key players entailed during DDR which are not only activated by DNA damage signals but also implicated in oxidative stresses 6 , 7 , and metabolic processes 8 , 9 . The dynamic stem cell microenvironment or the stem cell niche regulates stem cell fate through chemical ( secretory ) as well as physical cues necessary for survival and proliferation 10 ‚Äì 15 . Force transmission to the nucleus through the linker of nucleoskeleton and cytoskeleton ( LINC ) complex modulates expression of mechanosensitive genes through chromatin reorganization 16 ‚Äì 18 that directs stem cell differentiation 19 through extracellular matrix ( ECM ) stiffness 14 and topography 15 [ NO _ PRINTED _ FORM ] . Lamin A / C ‚Äì a critical component of the LINC complex , not only plays an important role in maintenance of genome integrity 20 ‚Äì 23 but also controls and contributes to nuclear mechanotransduction and stem cell fate determination 24 ‚Äì 27 . Extreme nuclear deformation has been shown to cause nuclear membrane rupture leading to genomic instability 28 ‚Äì 30 . Beyond their canonical DNA repair functions in somatic cells , recent studies have documented ATM / ATR activation in cells subjected to mechanical stresses 31 ‚Äì 34 . While alterations in ATR can cause nuclear envelope breakdown and aberrant chromatin organization 31 , 32 , loss in ATM triggers nuclear deformation and reduced Lamin A levels thus regulating nuclear stiffness and mechanics 35 . Given the association between extracellular matrix ( ECM ) stiffness and chromosomal instability in mouse ESCs ( mESCs ) 36 , in this study , we have probed the inter - relationships between ECM stiffness , DNA damage , DNA repair and mESC fate . By culturing mESCs on substrates of increasing stiffness , we show stiffness - dependent mechanoadaptation induces DNA damage through increased nuclear compression . This DNA damage in turn triggers stiffness dependent induction of Lamin A / C expression and early differentiation . Similar induction of DNA damage and Lamin A / C expression during chemical factor induced differentiation suggests DNA damage as an early event in the differentiation cascade . We further identify ATR as the regulator of Lamin A / C expression . Collectively , our study establishes stiffness - induced nuclear compression that mediates early ESC differentiation and ATR - dependent modulation of Lamin A / C expression . Results Stiffness - mediated mechanoadaptation is associated with DNA damage in mESCs Long - term culture of mESCs on stiff gelatin - coated coverslips have been shown to accumulate greater aneuploidy compared to that on mouse embryonic fibroblast ( MEF ) derived matrices , which are much softer 36 . To probe the link between stiffness - mediated mechanoadaptation and genomic integrity , we cultured mESCs on collagen ( Col ) and fibronectin ( FN ) coated 0 . 6 kPa , 4 kPa and 33 kPa PA gels that span a physiologically relevant stiffness range ( Fig . S1 ) . As mESCs exhibited greater spreading and focal adhesion intensities on FN coated gels compared to that on Col coated gels , indicative of a more robust mechanoadaptation response on FN coated gels ( Fig . S 1A , B ) , all subsequent experiments were performed on FN coated substrates up to 72 hrs in culture . While mESCs spread minimally on the softest 0 . 6 kPa gels , spreading increased substantially on the 4 and 33 kPa gels ( Fig . 1Ai , ii ) . Increased spreading on 4 and 33 kPa gels was associated with increased cytoskeletal organization as assessed by active actomyosin contractility ( phosphorylated myosin light chain , pMLC ) ( Fig . 1Bi , ii , Fig . S 2Ai ) and cortical stiffness ( Fig . 1Ci , ii ) . Interestingly stiffness - mediated mechanoadaptation was associated with stiffness - dependent increase in the extent of DNA damage assessed by counting the number of Œ≥H2AX foci in individual cells ( Fig . 1Di , ii ) , total protein levels assessed using westerns ( Fig . 1Diii , Fig . S 2Aii ) , as well as in comet assay where longer comet tails were observed in cells cultured on stiffer gels ( Fig . 1Ei , ii ) . Together , these results establish a direct relationship between stiffness - dependent mechanoadaptation and DNA damage in mESCs . DNA damage is induced by mechanoadaptation - mediated nuclear compression To test if stiffness - induced DNA damage was ROS dependent , DNA damage was assessed by quantifying Œ≥H2AX foci in cells cultured in the presence and absence of the ROS scavenger N - acetyl cysteine ( NAC ) . Insensitivity of DNA damage to NAC suggests that mechanoadaptation - mediated DNA damage in mESCs is ROS independent ( Fig . 2Ai , ii ) . Contractility - mediated nuclear compression has been shown to induce nuclear envelope rupture and DNA damage in cancer cells cultured on rigid glass coverslips 29 . To check the link between DNA damage and nuclear compression , we captured transverse sections of Lamin B1 - stained nuclei of mESCs cultured on different gels and quantified nuclear height . Increased DNA damage on stiffer substrates was associated with increased nuclear compression observed as early as 3 hrs ( Fig . 2Bi , ii ) . In comparison , nuclear volume increased marginally with time , but remained unchanged across different gels ( Fig . 2Biii ) . Based on these results , we propose that increased spreading and cytoskeletal organization on stiffer substrates induces DNA damage by nuclear compression through increased nucleo - cytoplasmic coupling . To test this , the extent of nuclear compression was perturbed by modulating cell spreading , and its impact on DNA damage assessed . While cells were treated with MnCl 2 on 0 . 6 kPa gels to increase cell spreading via increased integrin association 41 ‚Äì 43 , on 33 kPa gels , cells were treated with the contractility inhibitor blebbistatin ( Blebb ) ( Fig . 2Ci ) . On 0 . 6 kPa gels , MnCl 2 treatment led to increased spreading ( Fig . 2 Cii , iii ) , decreased nuclear height ( Fig . 2 Cii , iv ) and increased Œ≥H2AX foci ( Fig . 2 Di , ii ) . Conversely , on the stiff 33 kPa gels , treatment with Blebb led to decreased cell spreading ( Fig . 2 Cii , iii ) , increase in nuclear height ( Fig . 2 Civ ) and reduction in the extent of DNA damage ( Fig . 2 Di , ii ) . Western blotting of total cell lysates revealed increased pMLC and decreased Œ≥H2AX levels in MnCl 2 - treated cells , and the opposite in Blebb - treated cells ( Fig . 2 E , Fig . S 2 B ) . Collectively , these results demonstrate that mechanoadaptation - mediated DNA damage in mESCs is a consequence of nucleo - cytoplasmic coupling - induced nuclear compression . DNA damage induces loss of pluripotency and Lamin A / C expression at early time - points Lamin A / C is an integral part of the nuclear membrane that plays a key role in nuclear mechanics as well as in genome protection . ECM stiffness has been shown to drive differentiation of mesenchymal stem cells ( MSCs ) with induction of stiffness - dependent Lamin A / C expression 14 , 15 and affect ESC differentiation 44 , 45 . However , the importance of stiffness - induced DNA damage in this context remains unclear . Interestingly , increase in Lamin A / C levels on 4 kPa and 33 kPa gels and drop in pluripotency markers Oct3 / 4 and Nanog suggests that DNA damage and early differentiation may be closely related ( Fig . 3Ai , Aii , Fig . S 2 C ) . To test this , experiments were performed wherein DNA damage was induced with low doses of the topoisomerase II inhibitor ‚Äì etoposide ( ETO ) and its impact on pluripotency and Lamin A / C expression assessed . Remarkably , ETO treatment led to robust increase in Lamin A / C levels across all the gels with corresponding loss in Oct3 / 4 and Nanog expression , suggesting that low doses of DNA damage also induce loss in pluripotency and induction of Lamin A / C expression ( Fig . 3 Bi , Bii , Fig . S 2 D ) . To next probe if chemical factor induced differentiation also involves DNA damage , mESCs were cultured on the softest 0 . 6 kPa gels‚Äîwhere mechanoadaptation and DNA damage were lowest‚Äîin the presence of the neuronal differentiation factor retinoic acid ( RA ) and the osteogenic differentiation factor ascorbic acid ( AA ) while tracking DNA damage and Lamin A / C levels at 3 and 24 hr time - points . RA and AA treatments led to increased levels of the neuronal marker , Œ≤ - III tubulin at the 24 hr time - point ( Fig . S 2 E ) . Strikingly , both RA and AA treatment led to increase in Œ≥H2AX and Lamin A / C levels , and loss in Oct3 / 4 levels at the early 3 hr time - point ( Fig . 3 Ci , ii , Fig . S 2 F ) . Though Nanog was unchanged at the 3 hr time - point , near complete loss in its expression was observed at 24 hrs indicative of differentiation . Surprisingly , at this timepoint both Œ≥H2AX and Lamin A / C levels dropped to baseline levels . In comparison , no change in Lamin B1 levels was observed . To further probe the occurrence of DNA damage during mESC differentiation on more in - vivo mimetic conditions , we cultured mESCs on mouse embryonic fibroblast ( MEF ) derived matrices ( MEFDMs ) which induce mESC differentiation 46 ( Fig . 3Di , ii ) . In comparison to mESCs cultured on gelatin - coated glass coverslips in the absence of LIF ( serving as controls , CTL ) , in mESCs cultured on MEFDMs for 24 hrs , increase in Œ≥H2AX , loss in Oct3 / 4 levels and induction of Lamin A / C was observed ( Fig . 3 Diii , iv , Fig . S 2 G ) . While Nanog levels remained unchanged , surprisingly , loss in Lamin B1 was observed . In MEFs which possess high levels of Lamin A / C , no DNA damage was observed . Collectively , our results establish DNA damage as an early event in mESC differentiation that induces loss of pluripotency and Lamin A / C expression . RNAseq profiles of early responders of DNA damage revealed upregulation of ATR and RAD50 , downregulation of ATM , Mre11a , MDC1 , Trp53bp1 , RPA1 , RPA2 , Atrip , and upregulation of downstream DNA repair genes including Brca2 and Rbbp8 at 72 hr time - point . Fig . 5 B ) may correspond to resolution of DNA damage and mESC differentiation ( Fig . 5 A , B and C ) . Taken together , these results suggest that DNA damage - induced differentiation is associated with ATR mediated DDR activation followed by DNA repair . Furthermore , since RNAseq analysis of mESCs revealed an upregulation of mesodermal genes , we analyzed the effect of inhibiting DNA repair on development of the heart , a mesodermal organ , which is also mechanically active during its formation . The zebrafish heart forms as a tube and develops into a two - chambered contractile organ by 24 hours post fertilization ( hpf ) . The heart then undergoes morphogenetic movements resulting in a left - ward ventricle and right - ward atrium and chamber ballooning by ~ 72 hpf 78 . Zebrafish embryos exposed to ATM and ATR inhibitors ( KU55933 and VE821 respectively ) for 24 hours showed mild body curvature defects at 48 hpf ( Fig . S 6 Di , ii , n = 71 for KU and n = 73 embryos for VE ) and defects in chamber size and ballooning ( Fig . S 6 Dii ) . Following 24 hours exposure to inhibitors , larvae were allowed to recover in inhibitor - free medium for another 24 hours . However , the defects in heart morphogenesis worsened by 72 hpf , wherein the ventricle and atrium failed to attain their characteristic ballooning and left - right positioning in the larvae ( Fig . S 6 Diii , n = 35 for KU and n = 44 embryos for VE , Supp . movies S1 to S3 ) . Interestingly , in several embryos cardiac contractility was absent at 72 hpf ( 7 / 35 for KU and 2 / 44 for VE ) . These results suggest that ATM and ATR function is required for normal morphogenesis of the developing zebrafish heart and inhibition of ATM / ATR during cardiac morphogenesis causes inability of embryos to recover from the damage . ATR modulates Lamin expression Since both ATM and ATR have been linked to regulation of nuclear envelope integrity and lamin levels 31 , 35 , 79 , we probed the impact of ATM and ATR perturbations on lamin levels . To check whether downstream DDR pathway is blocked post KU and VE treatments , we assessed the Œ≥H2AX foci post 24 hrs of treatment and observed significant drop in Œ≥H2AX foci counts upon both ATM and ATR inhibition as compared to untreated controls ( Fig . 6 A and B ) , suggesting that early response to mechanoadaptation mediated DNA damage in mESCs is activated by both ATM and ATR . However , while ATM inhibition led to no significant reduction in Lamin A / C and Lamin B1 , both the lamins were significantly reduced upon ATR inhibition ( Fig . 6 A , C , Fig . S 6 E ) . Taken together , our observations suggests that during mechanoadaptation , lamin levels in mESCs are modulated through activation and nuclear localization of ATR and not ATM . Discussion Genome integrity has been associated with transmission of physical forces to the nucleus leading to nuclear envelope deformation , blebbing and rupture in cancer cells 28 , 29 . Lamin A / C is a crucial component of the nuclear envelope with Lamin A / C deficiency causing laminopathies and 80 , increased DNA damage 23 , 81 , 82 , and defective mechanotransduction 25 . Here , using FN coated PA gels of varying stiffness , we report stiffer matrices trigger greater DNA damage in mESCs through induction of nucleo - cytoplasmic coupling and nuclear compression . We show that DNA damage brings about loss in pluripotency and Lamin A / C induction at early time - points , thus functioning as a mediator of early differentiation . Differentiation of mESCs at the 72 hr time - point was associated with early induction of DNA damage response factors ( Œ≥H2AX , 53BP1 and pRPA2 ) and downstream DNA repair factors ( RAD51 ) . Of the two DNA damage response ( DDR ) factors ATM and ATR , we establish a role for ATR in modulating Lamin A / C and Lamin B1 expression . Collectively , our results demonstrate how stiffness - dependent DNA damage drives differentiation through ATR - dependent Lamin regulation ( Fig . 6 E ) . Stem cell maintenance and differentiation are regulated by several components of the stem cell niche . Cell - ECM interactions are known to regulate stem cell self - renewability and differentiation 83 . While collagen - I ( Col - I ) has been reported to maintain mESC self - renewability 84 , fibronectin ( FN ) has been associated with self - renewability as well as differentiation 85 ‚Äì 87 . MEFDMs of different composition , organization and stiffness have been shown to induce distinct mESC germ layer commitment 46 . In addition to its association with stem cell self - renewability and lineage commitment 14 , 26 , 45 , 88 , matrix stiffness has also been implicated in regulating genomic integrity ( ploidy ) of mESCs 36 . Our findings establish a direct link between stiffness mediated mechanoadaptation and DNA damage in mESCs on FN coated PA gels . While MSCs and cancer cells undergo nuclear compression , DNA damage and nuclear envelop rupture during confined migration 28 , 29 , 89 , 90 , we show for the first time that DNA damage in mESCs is attributed to stiffness - dependent nuclear compression . Increase in nuclear compression and DNA damage upon MnCl 2 treatment on the softest gels and decrease in nuclear compression and DNA damage upon Blebb treatment on the stiffest gels suggests that despite lacking a prominent acto - myosin network 91 , 92 , nucleocytoplasmic coupling is sufficient to induce DNA damage in mESCs . Consistent with a previous study where Lamin A / C was shown to have a direct correlation with tissue stiffness and MSC differentiation 26 , our results show a matrix stiffness dependent increase in Lamin A / C expression which is triggered by stiffness dependent DNA damage induction at 24 hr time - point . Similar loss of pluripotency factors and induction of Lamin A / C was observed in cells treated with ETO at 24 hrs . Remarkably , induction of DNA damage and Lamin A / C as early as 3 hrs in cells cultured in the presence of differentiating agents RA and AA suggests that DNA damage and induction of Lamin A / C expression are early events in mESC differentiation . Even in mESCs cultured on ~ 2 kPa MEFDMs wherein cells lose pluripotency within 24 hrs , DNA damage and higher Lamin A / C levels were observed . Drop in both DNA damage and Lamin A / C in RA / AA treated cells at 24 hrs suggests that Lamin A / C induction is DNA damage - dependent and temporal . Literature reports have documented opposite effects of DNA damage in inducing differentiation . While genotoxic DNA damaging agents have been shown to disrupt myogenic differentiation 93 , physiological DNA damage induced by caspase 3 has been shown to promote differentiation 94 . Moreover , confined migration has been reported to cause impaired cell cycle and delayed differentiation in myoblasts 95 . LIF maintains in - vitro mESC self - renewability through the STAT3 pathway 96 , and has been implicated as a mechano - inhibitor 36 . In mESCs , LIF withdrawal triggers an exit from pluripotency and induction of apoptosis 97 . However , mESCs on soft substrates maintain pluripotency even in absence of LIF by downregulation of traction forces 45 . Using RNA sequencing analysis and western blotting we show temporal downregulation of most stemness markers accompanied by upregulation of differentiation genes involved in germ layer commitment , in the absence of LIF . In line with previous observations 97 , 98 , temporal cell survivability assay on mESCs show ~ 40 % dead cells at 72 hr time - point . These observations suggests that a proportion of mESCs undergo cell death which could be triggered due to LIF withdrawal for longer duration . Since we also observed time dependent impairment of cell cycle and loss in mESC survivability with ~ 60 % Sub - G1 cells , the cells undergoing mixed lineage differentiation at the 72 hr time - point likely correspond to the surviving cells where DNA damage in resolved . Consistent with this , expression of the DNA repair protein RAD51 reduced at 72 hrs . Though there was loss of Lamin A / C at Day 3 , Lamin A / C levels were subsequently recovered on Day 7 . This is consistent with previous findings 27 , 71 illustrating the role of Lamin A / C oscillations in mESC differentiation . Interestingly , immuno - staining images revealed heterogenous Lamin A / C expression across all time - points . Analysis of Lamin A / C heterogeneity revealed 40 % Lamin A / C low cells at 72 hr time - point that correlates with the % dead cells from flow cytometric analysis of Annexin - PI . We speculate that restoration of lamin A / C levels also restores cell viability and constitutes the differentiating mESC population . Thus , matrix stiffness mediated DNA damage induces early Lamin A / C expression and loss in pluripotency at 24 hrs but a time - dependent Lamin A / C loss and restoration is required for non - aberrant mESC fate and differentiation . Early DNA damage response ( DDR ) factors ‚Äì ATM and ATR ‚Äì have been extensively linked to nuclear envelope integrity , regulation of lamins and mechanosensitivity 31 ‚Äì 33 , 35 , 79 . Our experiments in zebrafish embryos also show that ATM and ATR function is required for normal morphogenesis of the mechanically contractile zebrafish heart during critical stages of cardiac development . In mESCs , while pATM Ser1981 is uniformly present in nucleus and cytosol , here we show a temporal ATR activation and localization to the nucleus on stiff gels and its selective inhibition leading to lamin loss , thus illustrating the spatio - temporal role of pATR Thr1989 in modulation of both Lamin A / C and Lamin B1 in mESCs . However , apart from their canonical roles in regulation of DNA damage response , the DDR kinases , ATM and ATR have been reported to play roles in nuclear mechanotransduction and nuclear envelope ( NE ) integrity during cancer migration and invasion 31 , 32 , 34 , 35 , 79 . While ATM has been claimed to be activated by cell stretching 34 , ATR is activated by nuclear compression during interstitial migration 31 . Here , we report that the ATR kinase is activated upon matrix stiffness induced nuclear compression - mediated DNA damage that leads to modulation of lamin levels in mESCs . Taken together , our results indicate a novel role of matrix stiffness induced nuclear compression that triggers DNA damage in mediating ESC differentiation and ATR activation to the nucleus . In conclusion , our results illustrate the role of matrix stiffness in mediating nucleo - cytoplasmic coupling induced nuclear compression in mESCs leading to DNA damage . Our results also show that such DNA damage not only drives early differentiation and Lamin A / C induction but also activates and localizes ATR to the nucleus , indicative of the role of ATR in determination of lamin expression in mESCs during mechanoadaptation .
